🔌 Connect your SPOKE board

The SPOKE board is a special circuit board with 27 touch pins arranged in a circle. When you touch a pin — or connect something conductive to it — it tells the computer to play a sound.

What you need

🖥️ A computer
🔌 USB cable
🟢 SPOKE board
🌐 This webpage

How to connect

  1. Plug the USB cable into your SPOKE board
  2. Plug the other end into your computer
  3. Your computer will see it as a MIDI device — that's how the pins talk to this page
  4. Press the "Connect MIDI" button below
  5. Choose your SPOKE board from the list that pops up
Test it by touching a pin on the board — you'll see it light up on the spoke on the left!
Plug the USB cable from your computer into the SPOKE board

No board yet?

That's OK! You can still use this page with just your mouse or touchscreen. Tap the pins on the spoke to play sounds. You can also record your voice and search for sounds online.

✂️ Make your touch sensors

Each pin on the SPOKE board can sense when something conductive touches it. You're going to make your own touch sensors from craft materials and connect them with crocodile clips!

Materials

🐊 Crocodile clips
🪙 Tin foil
✏️ Soft pencil (6B)
🍌 Fruit / veg
📎 Paper clips
🎨 Card & paper
🧲 Conductive tape
🌿 Leaves / flowers

How it works

The SPOKE board sends a tiny, safe electrical signal through each pin. When you touch something conductive that's clipped to a pin, the signal flows through you and the board knows you've touched it!

Anything that conducts a little electricity will work — metal, fruit, wet things, graphite pencil marks, even your friends' hands!

Connecting a sensor

  1. Clip one end of a crocodile clip onto a pin on the SPOKE board
  2. Clip the other end onto your sensor material (foil, fruit, pencil drawing…)
  3. Touch the sensor — you should see the matching pin light up on screen!
Clip one end to a board pin, the other to your sensor — then touch it!

Sensor ideas for your story

  1. Foil lightning bolt — clip it to a thunder sound!
  2. Pencil-drawn footprints on card — footstep sounds when traced
  3. A real leaf — wind or forest sounds when touched
  4. A cup of water (dip a wire in) — splash or rain sounds
  5. Tin foil sword — sword clash sounds!
  6. A friend's hand! — hold hands to trigger a sound together
⚡ The signals are very tiny and completely safe — like the static from a balloon!

📖 Tell your story!

Everything is ready. Your sensors are connected, your sounds are loaded — now it's time to perform!

How to perform

  1. Tell your story out loud — use your voice to narrate
  2. When something happens in the story, touch the right sensor to play its sound
  3. You can have a friend be the sound operator while you narrate, or do both yourself!
  4. Try it a few times — practice makes a great performance!
Write your story out first and mark where each sound should happen. You could write the pin number next to each sound cue in your script!

Performance tips

  1. Timing is everything — pause your narration just before triggering a sound
  2. Let the audience see you touch the sensors — it's part of the magic!
  3. Invite the audience to touch sensors too — make it interactive!
