Chapter 1

The Journey Begins

It was a bright morning when Alex set out on their adventure. As they walked through the village, they could hear the footsteps on the cobblestone path. In the distance, a church bell rang out across the town.

The village soon gave way to rolling hills, where birds chirped happily in the trees. A gentle wind rustled through the grass.

Chapter 2

Into the Forest

The path led into a dense forest where sunlight filtered through the canopy. Every so often, a twig would snap underfoot. Deep in the woods, Alex heard the distant call of an owl.

A small stream bubbled nearby, its water crystal clear. Suddenly, thunder rumbled in the distance, followed by the patter of rain on the leaves above.

Chapter 3

The Mysterious Cave

Taking shelter from the rain, Alex discovered a cave entrance. Their voice echoed as they called out. Water dripped from stalactites high above.

In the darkness, Alex struck a match, creating a small flame. The light revealed ancient inscriptions on the walls. A mysterious magical glow began to emanate from deeper within.

🎨 Craft Challenge #3: Cave Elements

Chapter 4

The Treasure Chamber

Following the glow, Alex entered a grand chamber. In the center sat an ancient treasure chest. The rusty hinges creaked as it opened, revealing golden coins that jingled and sparkled.

But wait! A loud dragon roar echoed through the chamber! Alex quickly grabbed a magical sword from the chest. With a brave shout and a triumphant fanfare, the dragon was defeated!

🎨 Craft Challenge #4: Treasure & Action

Create exciting sensors! Use gold/yellow paper for treasure, red/orange for the dragon, silver foil for the sword. Make them big and dramatic! Connect to pads 13-18.

Chapter 5

The Journey Home

With treasure in hand, Alex emerged from the cave. The rain had stopped, and a beautiful rainbow appeared in the sky. Celebration music seemed to fill the air.

As the sun set, creating a peaceful ambiance, Alex walked back through the forest. The journey home was peaceful, with only the sound of crickets and a distant lullaby carried on the breeze.

And so, the adventure came to an end. The End.

✂️ How to Make Touch Sensors

Touch sensors work by detecting electrical conductivity. When you touch a conductive material, you complete a circuit!

📦 Materials You'll Need:

  • Aluminum foil (great for any shape!)
  • Copper tape (sticky and conductive)
  • Conductive fabric or thread
  • Cardboard or thick paper (base)
  • Crocodile clips (to connect to SPOKE)
  • Scissors, glue, decorations

🎨 Basic Touch Sensor Design

Step 1: Cut your cardboard into a fun shape (leaf, star, cloud, rock, etc.)

Step 2: Cover it with aluminum foil or copper tape

Step 3: Attach a crocodile clip to the foil/tape

Step 4: Connect the other end to a SPOKE pad

Step 5: Decorate! (but keep the conductive part accessible)

💡 Creative Ideas

  • Footsteps: Cut out shoe shapes from foil
  • Rain: Make cloud shapes with cotton and foil backing
  • Birds: Create bird silhouettes with wings spread
  • Dragon: Cut fierce dragon shape from red/orange paper + foil
  • Treasure: Make gold coin shapes or a treasure chest
  • Magic: Star shapes with glitter (foil inside)
  • Water: Wavy blue shapes representing streams
  • Thunder: Lightning bolt zigzag shapes

🔌 Connecting to SPOKE

Each SPOKE pad has a small hole or connection point. Here's how to connect:

  • Attach one crocodile clip to your sensor's foil
  • Attach the other end to the SPOKE pad connection
  • Make sure the connection is secure!
  • Test by touching your sensor - it should trigger the pad

Tip: Label your sensors so you remember which is which!
